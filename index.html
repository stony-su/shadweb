Chemical and Nano Technology Engineering Seminar: part 1

By Jesse Yeow

Waterloo professor and Rice graduate, Ting Tsui, explained the impacts of Nano Technology Engineering and potential career paths. Firstly, Nano Technology Engineering(NE) has a massive societal impact in the development of chips.Their impact is vast, enabling semiconductor integrated circuits in technology: from batteries and software modeling to medical imaging and even the COVID-19 vaccine. Imagine a world without chips: no social media, transportation, AI, or communication devices!" Secondly, Engineering in general is a very vast field. There are many paths which include: micro and nano-instrumentation, nano-electronics, nano-biosystems, and nano-engineered materials. Overall, 2% of Nano Technology Engineering graduates go to startups, 54% industry(jobs), 44% advanced studies(e.g. PH.D, MS, MD, MBA, JD, ETC.). Breaking down into specific fields, 21% of graduates enter software, 13% electronics, 12% finance, 12% manufacturing, 11% commercial engineering, 9% healthcare, 7% academic, 3% law, 3% design building, 2% environment, and 7% other fields.
